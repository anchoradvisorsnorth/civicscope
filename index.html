The Problem

Every other industry has tools for this. Local government has been left out. Until now.

The system left you with nowhere to start.

Local government employees exist to do one thing: help their communities. But when a project idea forms — a splash pad, a new fire station, a public works garage — the system gives you nowhere to turn.

Call a design firm? You're now in their process. They're sizing up your budget before you've asked a single question. Call a general contractor? Same thing. Spend hours researching online? You'll find national averages from years ago that mean nothing for your town, your project, your market.

Why This Exists
"The people responsible for delivering projects to their communities were often operating without clear answers at the moment it mattered most."

CivicScope was built by someone who came to the development world from the outside. Looking at the industry with fresh eyes made something obvious.

Before any calls are made, before consultants are engaged, before public commitments begin — there is a simple question that should come first: is this idea actually feasible? For many municipal teams, that question has never had a clear way to be answered.

The development process evolved around design firms, consultants, and contractors who are typically the first call. The people representing the public often enter the process later, when key assumptions are already set.

CivicScope was built to change that starting point — giving municipal leaders a clearer understanding of feasibility before the process begins. For the parks director exploring a new facility. For the clerk reviewing a proposal. For the council member asking a straightforward question: is this worth pursuing?
