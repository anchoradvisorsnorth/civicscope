    Understanding Your Path to Getting This Built

    The procurement path you choose affects your total cost, timeline, and how much risk your municipality carries.

    Traditional Path

    Design-Bid-Build

    • Design and engineering fees paid before any commitment — typically 8–12% of project cost
    • Public bid advertising required — 12–18 months from design start to groundbreaking
    • Legally required to accept the lowest bidder regardless of track record
    • Change orders average 11% cost growth after award
    On a project in this range, design fees and change order risk can add significantly to your final cost.
    Alternative Path

    BOT / P3 Delivery

    • No upfront design spend. Receive a Guaranteed Maximum Price before committing to any design dollars
    • Developer, designer, and contractor work together from day one — value engineering before costs are locked
    • Indiana BOT statute enables GMP procurement — no low-bid requirement
    • Cost overrun risk stays with the developer, not your municipality
    Developer fee ~4% of project cost. No change orders. Budget certainty from day one.
