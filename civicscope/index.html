    Understanding Your Path to Getting This Built

    The procurement path you choose affects your total cost, your timeline, and how much risk your municipality carries

    Traditional Path

    Plan-Spec-Low Bid

    • Design and engineering fees paid before any commitment — typically 8–12% of project cost spent before a shovel hits the ground
    • Public bid advertising required — 12–18 months from design start to groundbreaking
    • Legally required to accept the lowest bidder regardless of track record or fit
    • Change orders average 11% cost growth after award on public low-bid projects
    • If bids exceed budget, design fees are spent with nothing to show
    On a project in this range, design fees and change order risk can add significantly to your final cost.
    Alternative Path

    Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT) / P3

    • No upfront design spend. Receive a Guaranteed Maximum Price before committing to design dollars
    • Design and contractor teams engaged together from day one — maximizing value engineering before costs are locked
    • In Indiana, BOT statute allows GMP procurement — no low-bid requirement
    • No change orders — cost overrun risk stays with the developer, not your municipality
    • Privately delivered — reduced administrative burden on municipal staff throughout the project
    Developer fee ~4% of project cost. No change orders. Budget certainty from day one.
